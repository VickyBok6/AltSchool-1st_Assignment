AltSchool Africa is an innovative educational institution focused on bridging the skills gap in Africa by offering specialized training in technology. Founded with the mission of empowering the next generation of African tech talent, AltSchool Africa provides accessible and affordable education in software engineering, data science, product design, and other tech-related fields. The core aspects of AltSchool Africa include:

  1. Curriculum: The curriculum is designed to be practical and industry-relevant, focusing on hands-on learning and real-world applications. Students work on projects that mimic real industry scenarios, which helps them build a strong portfolio.

  6. Partnerships: AltSchool Africa collaborates with tech companies and industry professionals to ensure the curriculum is up-to-date with the latest trends and technologies. These partnerships also provide students with internship and job placement opportunities.
  7. Focus on In-Demand Skills: The programs at AltSchool Africa focus on skills that are in high demand in the tech industry, such as software engineering, data science, and product design. This ensures that graduates are well-prepared for the job market.

From promoting the development of democratic institutions, to establishing peace between warring nations, the UN is present on the ground supporting economic and social development and the promotion and protection of human rights. In this effort, the UN works closely with Africa’s regional cooperation mechanisms and has five active peacekeeping operations at present. UN peacekeepers serve in the Central African Republic (MINUSCA), the Democratic Republic of the Congo (MONUSCO), Western Sahara (MINURSO), South Sudan (UNMISS) and in the disputed Abyei area (UNISFA). To advance its support for Africa even further, the United Nations Office of the Special Adviser on Africa was established in 2003 to enhance international support for African development and security and to improve coordination of UN system support. It also works to facilitate global deliberations on Africa, particularly with respect to the New Partnership for Africa’s Development (NEPAD) — a strategic framework adopted by African leaders in 2001. In 2018, NEPAD's mandate was reformed and transformed into the African Union Development Agency-NEPAD (AUDA-NEPAD).
